Disclaimer

This game's scenarios are fictional. Although the diseases and its descriptions in the game are real, the "cures" and simple treatment methods of them are oversimplified and should not be used as a guide to cure them in real life. Many diseases back in the day have had no clear treatments and if so, had extreme side effects that ended up worsening the condition. The "cures" were simplified for the purpose of this game.


Getting Started

Ahoy mateys! Welcome to Castaway Cure: KAPS Pirate Rescue! A mighty mission has been bestowed upon you, and it is up to you, the Pirate Doctor, to rescue your mateys and find a castaway cure before it is too late!


Goal

To save as much shipmates and strangers as possible using your limited resources and found inventory


Inventory

At the start of the game, you are given basic necessities and inventory to help you concoct the perfect cure based on the disease of your patients. You only have a limited amount, so use it wisely. If you run out, you will have to dock on the deserted island to find your required supplies.


Mission

For every mission you complete, you get points. Each mission gives different points. However, you need to do it correctly, or else your points would get deducted.


Diseases Guide in the Game

  1. Scurvy

    2. Scurvy is vitamin C deficiency due to the lack of fresh vegetables and fruits in their diet. Symptoms included weakness, swollen gums, joint pain, and skin issues.

    Cure: 2 lemon, 1 lime, 1 orange

  2. Dysentery

    3. Dysentery is inflammation or infection of the intestines causing diarrhea with blood or mucus, often caused by shigella bacteria (shigellosis). Symptoms included diarrhea, abdominal pain, fever, and dehydration.

    Cure: 1 plant, 2 bananas

  3. Rickets

    4. Rickets is a condition caused by vitamin D deficiency, leading to weakened bones and deformities. Symptoms included weak bones, stunted growth, and skeletal deformities.

    Cure: Fish oil

  4. Food poisoning

    5. Foodborne illnesses and food poisoning could occur due to spoiled or contaminated food and water. Symptoms included nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, and dehydration.

    Cure: 2 charcoal


On a serious note!

The life of a pirate, though often romanticized in popular culture, was fraught with a variety of common health issues. These issues resulted from the harsh conditions, poor diet, and close quarters of life at sea. Some of the health problems frequently experienced by pirates included:

  1. Scurvy

    2. Scurvy was a prevalent and potentially life-threatening condition among pirates due to their limited access to fresh fruits and vegetables during long sea voyages. The lack of vitamin C in their diets led to symptoms such as fatigue, gum disease, and joint pain.

  2. Malnutrition

    3. The limited and monotonous diet aboard pirate ships often resulted in malnutrition. Pirates typically consumed preserved and salted foods, which lacked essential nutrients and contributed to various health problems.

  3. Dehydration

    4. Staying well-hydrated was a constant challenge at sea, and pirates frequently suffered from dehydration due to inadequate access to fresh water. Dehydration could lead to fatigue, dizziness, and other health issues.

  4. Infections and Wounds

    5. Life on a pirate ship was hazardous, and injuries were common. Infections resulting from wounds or poor sanitation were significant health concerns, as there were limited medical resources available.

  5. Tooth Decay and Gum Diseases

    6. Poor oral hygiene and a diet high in sugar contributed to rampant tooth decay and gum disease among pirates.

  6. Skin Conditions

    7. Prolonged exposure to harsh sun and saltwater could lead to various skin conditions, including sunburn, rashes, and fungal infections.

  7. Mental Health

    8. The stressful and violent nature of pirate life, along with the challenges of isolation at sea, took a toll on the mental health of many pirates. Depression, anxiety, and alcohol addiction were not uncommon

  8. Physical Injuries

    9. Pirates engaged in frequent battles and confrontations, resulting in physical injuries such as cuts, bruises, broken bones, and even dismemberment.
